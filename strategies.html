Overview

There is a lot of differents strategies for Mikii, both for ARS and SRS. I will not be covering them all, but i will talk about the ones i personally think are the most viable ways to beat the game. You can see most (if not all) strategies to beat Mikii on Greentea's channel.

Strategies vary a lot based on difficulty, speed, medals required. Depending on whether you just want to get a 1300-2600 run, get GM, or aim for high GM-Rounds grades, you might want to choose one strategy over another.

I will showcase all of the strategies I think are worth learning the most.

Combo funnel (Yoshi100_Aus' strat)

The strategy was made by Yoshi100_Aus. It focuses on burning pieces in a widening funnel, starting from a 1 wide, slowly transitioning into a 4 wide. It is a variation of what people call a "tower" strat.

Pros

  • - Can achieve very fast times.
  • - Many, many ways to get rid of residue in the well.

Cons

  • - Requires a good amount of medals.
  • - Hard to learn.
  • - You do not gain medals during execution, making END GAME harder.

In my opinion this is the best strategy up to date, if you want to get the highest grades possible. Might be a bit overkill to learn if you just want to get a 2600 run though.

1-2-3 wide (Greentea's strat)

Another "tower" strat. This one was made by Greentea and involves starting with a tetris well, transitioning into a 2x2 box, allowing to score tetrises while silmultaneously burning pieces on the side.

Pros

  • - Easier to learn than combo funnel.
  • - No need for as many medals as combo funnel
  • - Gains medals during execution which makes END GAME easier.

Cons

  • - Slower than combo funnel.
  • - Quite a bit of piece sequences are unsolvable

This is the strat you'd probably want to go with if you want to get your first GM, as you don't have to learn as many setups as combo funnel. Could also be used as a backup strategy to the combo funnel strat if your transition into 1300 is messy.

Freestyle/Korean/Line-out

A lot of different names for that one. But the core concept is the following: burn lines as fast as possible. That's it. You just have to outpace the ice.

Pros

  • - You don't have to learn any setups.
  • - Looks cool.

Cons

  • - Extremely hard.
  • - Extremely slow.
  • - Requires an insane amount of medals.

Probably the first strat that comes to mind when you try Mikii for the first time. Requires a crazy amount of skill and medals to pull off in return of... being slower and getting lower GM-Rounds grades. You might not even make the 2600 torikan with this strat. I still recommend to try it out though, helps out a lot with mastering Cyclone. And it's fun!

Hybrid (Freestyle+Tetris)

The name speaks for itself. You build a regular tetris well, but you do freestyle stacking on top of it, trying to maintain height while scoring tetrises when possible.

Pros

  • - You don't have to learn any setups.
  • - More forgiving than pure freestyle.
  • - Gains medals during execution which makes END GAME easier.

Cons

  • - Harder than tower strats.
  • - Requires a good amount of medals.
  • - Not as fast as tower strats.

The closest you will get to regular Tetris - a realistic alternative to pure freestyle. Doesn't present much value though, in my opinion. Harder than tower strats without being as fast.
