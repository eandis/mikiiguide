Greentea's 1-2-3 Wide

← Back to Guide

  • All Clears: 0
  • Tetrises: As much as possible
  • T-Spins: 0
  • Pikii: less than 29

You want to have low freezing time to have enough room to set up your 1-2-3 wide, while still trying to be fast, thus a lot of Tetrises and low Pikii count is required. If you want to get the level bonus from Pikii, you will have to get it to 28, otherwise you can keep it as low as you want.

Unlike Combo Funnel or Freestyle, this strategy does not neccesarily require you to have a low stack height when transitioning into 1300. It is entirely possible to enter with a high messy stack, build out the 1-2-3 wide, and then clean up with tetrises afterwards.

The main idea of the strat is to build a 1 wide tetris on the left side, then transition into a "2x2 box" and high enough 3 wide (at least 2 high). That allows you to burn certain piece combos in that "box" while clearing Tetrises.

There are a few ways to set that up. The most commons ones are using the L piece, or the O piece.

Rebuilding the well

No matter how hard you try, eventually after enough Tetrises your 1 wide well will become low enough to the point that you can't score Tetrises anymore. At that point you need to rebuild it. Your goal is to get column 9 and 8 to the same height, get them to at least 4 cells high (to be Tetris ready) and from there do the usual 2x2 setup. This is exactly why we want fast freezing time. If you have slow freezing time, the pieces in the 2x2 box won't be able to freeze in time, thus you won't be able to skim in it.

Due to collecting a ton of medals throughout the run, the speed at which you have to play END GAME is just a bit higher than the speed you played at on the first lap.

A very crucial part of END GAME is to not manlock during the rebuilding phase, as you the pieces freeze slower from all the medals you've collected, and you might end up trying to skim way too early.

For a safe completion, if you don't lock your pieces manually during the first lap, you should have plenty enough time after rewind. You don't even have to manually lock the entire END GAME portion, just try to manlock a somewhat significant portion of it.

For reference: Greentea on his GMR-10 run during END GAME manually locked pieces around ~650 levels from total ~1030 levels, which is just a bit over half the time and still had 10 seconds remaining.
